For working individuals and families experiencing homelessness, finding a safe and affordable place to live while they transition out of poverty can be a challenge.

The STAR Transitional Program helps people who are homeless, despite having a job, find a safe and encouraging community where they can transition out of homelessness and into a sustainable, self-sufficient life. At the Mission’s largest facility, The Crossing, individuals and families are able to live in a safe and encouraging community away from the substance abuse prevalent in hotels and on the streets.

 

Making the Transition

We help STAR Transitional Program participants through:

  • Case management
  • Life-skills training
  • Financial management training
  • Cultivating healthy community relationships
  • Youth development programs

Unlike the New Life Program, the STAR Program uses program fees to encourage personal and financial responsibility. At least one STAR Transitional Program adult must have a minimum income of $800/month for individuals and $1,000/month for families.
